Cauliflower Tacos

Ingredients
½ head cauliflower, cut into florets
2 cups (328 g) cooked chickpeas
1 medium sweet potato, peeled, thinly sliced
1 red bell pepper, sliced
½ cup (120 mL) barbecue sauce
1 tsp (2 g) ground cumin
1 tsp (3 g) garlic powder
1 tsp (2 g) cayenne pepper
½ tsp smoked sweet paprika powder

Directions
1. Preheat the oven to 390°F (200°C) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
2. Add all the traybake ingredients to the baking tray and mix well.
3. Bake on the center rack of the preheated oven for 40 - 50 minutes, or until the veggies are tender. Stir at least once halfway.
4. Meanwhile, make the yogurt sauce by mixing together the yogurt and ranch seasoning in a small bowl.
5. Once the traybake is done baking, add the remaining BBQ sauce and stir to coat. Serve inside the warmed tortillas, topped with sliced avocado, onion, and the ranch sauce. Enjoy!

Jackfruit Noodles

Ingredients
Stir-fry
1 bell pepper, sliced
2 cups (356 g) canned young green jackfruit, roughly chopped
1 Tbsp (15 mL) vegetable oil
14 oz (400 g) pre-cooked udon noodles
1 stalk green onion, sliced, light and dark green parts separated
Sauce
½ cup (120 mL) canned light coconut milk
⅓ cup (80 mL) water
⅓ cup (80 mL) peanut butter, plus more to taste
3 Tbsp (45 mL) sodium-reduced soy sauce
1 Tbsp (15 mL) sesame oil
½ vegetable bouillon cube, crushed
½ lime, juiced
1 clove garlic, crushed
2 tsp (5 g) cornstarch
2 tsp (8 g) brown sugar, lightly packed
2 tsp (10 g) crispy chili oil
1 tsp (2 g) freshly grated ginger (optional)

Directions
1. To a sauté pan on medium-high heat, cook the bell pepper and jackfruit in the oil for 3 - 4 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, combine the sauce ingredients in a jar.
3. Add the udon noodles and the white and light green parts of the green onion to the pan. Gently break the noodles apart, cooking for 2 minutes.
4. Pour the sauce into the pan and cook until everything is well coated in the sauce.
5. Garnish with the dark green parts of the green onion, and add any other desired toppings. Enjoy!

Garlic Chili Noodles
Ingredients
7.1 oz (200 g) dry Taiwanese flat noodles
2 Tbsp (30 mL) vegetable oil, neutral tasting
6 cloves garlic, minced
1 stalk green onion, sliced, dark green parts separated
1 Tbsp (8 g) gochugaru
2 Tbsp (30 mL) dark soy sauce
1 tsp (5 mL) black rice vinegar
6 heads of baby bok choy
2 cups (200 g) frozen shelled edamame beans

Directions
1. In a large pot, cook the noodles according to the package instructions, for us this took 3 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, add the oil to a large pan on medium-high heat. When hot, add the garlic and light parts of the green onion. Cook for 2 minutes. Then add the gochugaru and cook for 1 minute longer.
3. Drop the heat to low and add the soy sauce, vinegar, and use tongs to add the noodles from the pot to the pan, without draining the cooking water. Mix everything together.
4. Add the bok choy and edamame to the pot of boiling water in which the noodles were cooked. Bring to a boil and cook for 3 minutes, or until cooked to your liking. Then drain.
5. Serve the noodles in bowls, with the bok choy served alongside them. Garnish with the dark green part of the green onion and sesame seeds if desired. Serve the edamame on the side with a sprinkle of salt overtop. Enjoy!

Tofu Tikka Masala
Ingredients
2 Tbsp (30 mL) vegetable oil, divided
8.8 oz (250 g) firm tofu, cut into 2 cm pieces
1 medium onion, chopped
1 Tbsp (6 g) freshly grated ginger
1 tsp (2 g) whole brown mustard seeds
2 Tbsp (12 g) tikka masala spice mix
½ tsp salt
¼ tsp chili powder, or less for less spice
1 large tomato, chopped
¾ cup (180 mL) soy cooking cream
½ cup (120 mL) unsweetened soy yogurt
¼ cup (32 g) raw cashews, chopped
3 Tbsp (49 g) tomato paste
½ lime, juiced
2 vegan naans

Directions
1. To a large pan on high heat, add half of the oil and cook the tofu, turning it periodically so it gets lightly golden on all sides, about 8 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, to another large pan on medium-high heat, add the remaining oil and cook the onion for 3 minutes.
3. Add the ginger and mustard seeds. Cook for 2 more minutes.
4. Add the masala seasoning, salt, and chili and toast for another minute. The dish is spicy, so omit or add less chili if you're not a fan of heat.
5. Add the chopped tomato, cooking cream, yogurt, cashews, and tomato paste. Cook for 7 - 8 minutes.
6. Lastly, add the cooked tofu and stir it through.
7. Squeeze over the lime juice, stir, and serve with naan or rice. Enjoy!

Spicy Orange Tofu
Ingredients
Tofu
11 oz (325 g) firm tofu, pressed
1 Tbsp (15 mL) olive oil
1 Tbsp (8 g) cornstarch
½ tsp salt
Rice & veggies
½ cup (100 g) uncooked white rice
7.1 oz (200 g) broccolini
1 red bell pepper, sliced
Orange glaze
1 cup (240 mL) orange juice
2 cloves garlic, crushed
1 Tbsp (15 mL) sriracha hot sauce, or less if you prefer less spicy
2 Tbsp (30 mL) sodium-reduced soy sauce
½ Tbsp (3 g) freshly grated ginger
½ Tbsp (7 mL) rice vinegar
1 Tbsp (8 g) cornstarch
1 tsp (5 mL) agave syrup, plus more to taste

Directions
1. Preheat the oven to 390°F (200°C). Cut the pressed tofu into ½ inch (1 cm) cubes.
2. In a large bowl, gently mix together the tofu, oil, cornstarch, and salt.
3. Spread the tofu evenly over a parchment-lined baking tray and bake in the oven on the centre rack for 20 - 25 minutes, flipping the pieces halfway through baking.
4. Cook the rice according to the package instructions.
5. To a large pan, add the broccolini and bell pepper with a small splash of water. Cover and cook for 3 - 4 minutes or until softened slightly. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
6. Returning the now-empty pan to the stove, add the orange glaze ingredients. Whisk. Once whisked, heat* up the sauce and let simmer for 4 - 5 minutes, stirring often until thickened.
7. Add the baked tofu cubes to the pan and stir to coat. Cook until most of the liquid has been absorbed by the tofu.
8. Serve with rice and veggies, garnish as desired, and enjoy!

Couscous Salad
Ingredients
1 cup (173 g) dry couscous
½ vegetable bouillon cube, crushed
1 cup (240 mL) boiling water
1 cup (149 g) cherry tomatoes
½ cucumber
⅓ cup (45 g) kalamata olives
3.2 oz (90 g) vegan feta
3 Tbsp (4 g) fresh basil (optional)
3 Tbsp (6 g) fresh mint leaves (optional)
3 Tbsp (3 g) fresh parsley (optional)
¾ cup (148 g) cooked brown lentils
¼ cup (64 g) vegan green pesto

Directions
1. To a large bowl, add the couscous, crushed bouillon cube, and boiling water. Mix, cover with a plate and set aside.
2. In the meantime, chop the veggies, olives and feta into bite-sized pieces, and finely chop the herbs.
3. Coming back to the large bowl with couscous, remove the plate and use a fork to “fluff” the couscous.
4. Add the veggies, feta, herbs, lentils and pesto to the bowl with couscous. Mix and enjoy!

Pasta Salad
Ingredients
3 cups (225 g) dry fusilli pasta
2 red bell peppers
½ cucumber
¾ cup (12 g) fresh dill
½ cup (68 g) kalamata olives
¼ medium red onion, minced, plus more to taste
1½ cups (246 g) cooked chickpeas

Directions
1. Cook the pasta per packaging instructions until al dente, about 8 minutes. Then drain and set aside.
2. Meanwhile, chop the bell peppers, cucumber, dill, olives, and mince the red onion.
3. Add all the dressing ingredients to a bowl and whisk. If the dressing is too thick, add a splash of water. Taste test, and add more lemon juice, garlic, or salt if desired.
4. To a large bowl, add all the salad ingredients. Pour over the dressing and toss to coat. Garnish as desired. Enjoy!​

Hummus Pasta Bake
Ingredients
Pasta
½ medium broccoli
14 oz (400 g) dry shell pasta
1½ cups (224 g) cherry tomatoes
1⅔ cups (399 mL) canned full-fat coconut milk
¾ cup + 1 Tbsp (200 g) hummus
Vegan parmesan cheese
½ cup (65 g) raw cashews
2 Tbsp (4 g) nutritional yeast flakes
¼ tsp garlic powder (optional)
⅛ tsp salt

Directions
1. Cut the outer thick skin off the stem of the broccoli and discard. Cut the stem into little pieces. Cut the broccoli florets into bite-sized pieces.
2. Cook the pasta in salted boiling water for 4 - 5 minutes. Preheat the oven to 390°F (200°C).
3. Then add the chopped broccoli stem and florets to cook with the pasta. After 1 minute, drain. We want the pasta to be slightly under-cooked.
4. Transfer the drained pasta and broccoli to a large 11 x 8 inch (28 x 21 cm) baking dish, along with the tomatoes.
5. In a medium bowl, mix together the coconut milk and hummus. Pour this over the pasta and toss everything to coat. Bake in the centre rack of the preheated oven for 20 minutes.
6. Meanwhile, add the cashews, nutritional yeast, garlic powder, and salt to a small food processor*. Blend into a coarse powder.
6. If desired, although optional, add some of the cashew 'Parmesan' cheese to the top of the baked pasta and bake for an additional 3 - 5 minutes to toast.
7. Serve the pasta while hot, and sprinkle over top more vegan 'Parmesan' cheese. Enjoy!

Cauliflower Pizza
Ingredients
Pizza dough
2½ cups + 2 Tbsp (328 g) self-rising flour
1 cup (240 mL) water
2 Tbsp (30 mL) olive oil
1 tsp (6 g) salt
BBQ cauliflower ½ head cauliflower, broken into florets
¾ cup (180 mL) barbecue sauce (or less for a milder BBQ taste)
½ tsp garlic powder
½ tsp onion powder
Pizza sauce
¾ cup (184 g) hummus
⅓ cup (87 g) tomato paste
1 tsp (1 g) dried oregano
1 tsp (1 g) dried basil
½ tsp dried thyme
½ tsp salt
¼ tsp ground black pepper

Directions
1. Combine all of the pizza dough ingredients in a large bowl and mix until well combined.
2. Transfer to a floured surface and knead until you have a smooth, firm dough. Add a sprinkle of flour if too wet, or a small splash of water if too dry.
3. Cover the dough with a clean kitchen towel and let it rest for about 20 minutes.
4. Preheat the oven to 430°F (220°C). Add the cauliflower florets to a lined baking tray.
5. In a small bowl, mix together the BBQ sauce, garlic powder, and onion powder.
6. Pour half of the sauce over the florets, and toss to coat. Reserve the other half of the BBQ sauce for later. Bake the florets for 20 minutes, flipping once halfway.
